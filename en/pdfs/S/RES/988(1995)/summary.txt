
The Security Council adopted Resolution 988 (1995) to suspend restrictions and other measures on the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (Serbia and Montenegro) until July 5th, 1995. The resolution also calls for the investigation of reports of helicopter flights crossing the border between the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (Serbia and Montenegro) and the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina, and requests Member States to make resources available to strengthen the International Conference on the Former Yugoslavia (ICFY) Mission's capacity to carry out its tasks. The resolution also reaffirms the decision that import, export, and transshipment to and from the United Nations Protected Areas in the Republic of Croatia and those areas of the Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina under the control of Bosnian Serb forces shall be permitted only with proper authorization.
