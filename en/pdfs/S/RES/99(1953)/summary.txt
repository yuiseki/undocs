 The Security Council discussed the recommendation for the appointment of the Secretary-General and requested the permanent members to hold consultations and report back. At its 617th meeting, the Council decided to recommend Mr. Dag Hammarskjold for the position. Additionally, the Council voted to accept the resignation of Judge Sergei Alexandrovich Golunsky and adopted resolutions or decisions on the matter. On 27 November 1953, the Security Council and the General Assembly elected Mr. Feodor Ivanovich Kozhevnikov to fill the vacancy and referred the applications of Japan and the Republic of San Marino to become parties to the Statute of the International Court of Justice to the Committee of Experts for study and report.
