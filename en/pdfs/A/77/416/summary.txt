 The United Nations General Assembly is considering a draft resolution on crimes against humanity. The resolution was introduced by the Gambia on behalf of a group of countries and was adopted without a vote. The resolution expresses appreciation to the International Law Commission for its contribution to the codification and progressive development of international law, takes note of the draft articles on prevention and punishment of crimes against humanity, and decides that the Sixth Committee shall resume its session for five days in April 2023 and six days in April 2024 to exchange views on the draft articles and consider further the recommendation of the Commission.
